Thesis Weekly Update #9

April 1, 2024: Project Documentation Peer Review

Relevant Focus Areas

In the remainder of the semester, I’ll be focusing more on developing the learning scaffold for novice tarot readers performing a simple 3-card predictive reading. I spent this week focusing on the step-by-step approach for this scaffold, which will be an activity between two people who take turns reading for each other. In the final version, the activity would be formatted with these instructions:

  1. Before they start: Both participants watch a brief 30-60s video of a sample predictive tarot reading to know what to expect and how a reading may be conducted.
  2. Reader asks the querent to come up with an open-ended question they’d like to know the outcome of.
  3. Querent thinks of their question. Reader writes down the question on the worksheet.
  4. Reader has querent knock on the top of the deck three times.
  5. Reader asks querent to provide some more context about their inquiry, why they’d like to know the outcomes, and how they’re feeling about the situation AS they (reader) shuffle the deck.
  6. Reader has 2 options: fan the deck out in a straight line OR split the deck into 3 piles.
  7. Reader pulls three cards and situates them in a row: leftmost Past, middle Present, rightmost Future. The cards should all be face down.
  8. Reader flips over each card from left to right. Take a moment to observe the cards and note your own reactions.
  9. On the worksheet, reader writes the names of all 3 cards and marks the 3 hand gestures they observe. Reader may use the bottom yellow space to track their own notes.
  10. Reader tells the narrative they’re observing, using the hand gesture keys and their own interpretation of the imagery. ***Be sure to go one card at a time*** - Past card first, Present card second, Future card third.
  11. Allow input from querent, such as their reactions or any follow-up questions they have.
  12. Reader can now flip to the relevant description pages in the booklet. On the worksheet, fill out the keywords from the booklet (or substitute with your own). Reader may add more notes to the bottom yellow space.
  13. Reader provides more context or fills in any gaps that the querent identified.
  14. Reader concludes the reading. Organize the cards back into a single pile and have the querent knock on the top of the deck to clear its energy. Thank the querent for participating in the reading.
  15. Now switch! Return to step 2.

I’ll be playtesting with people over the next two weeks. Five participants reached out expressing interest to playtest, one of whom was an expert that I interviewed last semester. In addition I’ll be playtesting on Thursday at NYU.

Paper Approach

In addition to working on the learning scaffold, I’ve been chipping away at my process paper. I plan to follow this structure:

  1. Introduction
  2. Background Research
    • Brief cultural history of tarot, popular decks, readings, tarot in educational settings, maybe commercialization of tarot
    • Learning and multimedia design frameworks, cognitive science theory
  3. Developing a Framework for Tarot Learning
    • Field Research insights - photo study, expert interviews, learner/stakeholder interviews
    • Research Framework Design - mood boards, research compilation of card meanings
    • Establishing an Initial Approach - sticky note exercise to develop my own card meanings
  4. Designing the Cups and Pentacles
    • Decision for splitting the Minor Arcana
    • Developing the hand gesture key system based off these two suits
  5. Designing the Wands and Swords
    • Designing the suits with an established key system
    • Designing the narrative booklet
  6. Designing the Instructional Guidance for Tarot Reading
    • Ideating with learning design frameworks
    • Playtesting and insights
  7. Final Design and Reflections
    • Revising all materials - instructional guidance, tarot cards, and booklet
    • Key takeaways
  8. Next Steps
    • Designing Major Arcana and Court cards
    • Web experience?

I met with Professor Piantella last week to get some feedback on my thesis progress and any resources that would be helpful for literature review. She suggested looking into scaffolding models of learning and cognitive science literature. Some examples of topics to look at are information processing models, apprenticeship learning systems, experiential learning, assimilation theory, principles in multimedia design, coherence theory, signaling principle, and Mayer’s principles (specifically personalization, pre-training, and contiguity).

I think I may have the most trouble interweaving secondary research effectively. I discussed this with Professor Piantella and she recommended having sub sections in my background research for tarot and learning design. And then I could reference back to my learning design citations in later sections of the process paper, especially in the instructional guidance section.

I’m a little nervous about having the final version of my cards ready before I print them. I will probably do it locally in NYC, because I might not have enough turnaround time for longer distance shipping if I’m playtesting this week and next. I think it will all work out though!
