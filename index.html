Perspective Tarot Deck: Designing Tarot for Novice Readers

Project Description

For Thesis, I will be designing my own tarot deck, as well as accompanying learning materials, specifically catered to novice tarot readers. My personal goal for this project is to explore ways of facilitating text-and-image based learning. In addition, I'm eager to build a website that guides users towards drawing interrelated connections between cards in a layout, while still empowering them to trust their intuition. As such, my project will focus on faciliating the learning of individual cards as well as contextual narrative readings.

My final deliverables will consist of:

  • A 78-card tarot deck
  • An accompanying booklet with each card's description
  • A website that guides users to read layouts independently
  • A workbook with daily prompts for the first month of learning

What is Tarot?

Tarot is a pack of 78 cards, split between the Major and Minor Arcanas, that describe secrets and insights regarding life scenarios. It’s popularly used as a tool of divination or predicting the future, but people also use Tarot to gain clarity into life events or questions.

The Major Arcana consists of 22 cards that follow the Fool’s journey from Earth into the divine realm. The Minor Arcana is split into 4 suits (Cups, Pentacles, Swords, Wands), each comprised of 14 cards (numbered 1-10, Page, Knight, Queen, and King).

The Cups generally represent emotions and relationships, Pentacles represent money and health, Swords represent troubles and problems, and Wands represent communication and ideas.
